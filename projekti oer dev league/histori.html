About the Museum

The National Museum of Kosovo, established in 1949, is the oldest cultural heritage institution in the country. It aims to preserve, restore, and present Kosovo's rich history and culture.

History of the Museum

The museum is located in a Neo-Baroque building constructed between 1885 and 1886, originally serving as the Pristina Government Office. This historic building reflects the modernization wave of the late Ottoman Empire and has been repurposed to showcase Kosovo's cultural heritage.

With over 50,000 exhibits, the museum's collections include artifacts from the Neolithic era to modern times, highlighting periods such as the Illyrian, Dardanian, and Roman eras. A standout piece is the “Goddess on the Throne,” a 6,000-year-old terracotta statue.

Exhibitions

Explore permanent and temporary exhibitions that showcase the rich history and culture of Kosovo. Highlights include archaeological findings, ethnographic displays, and natural science exhibits.

Archaeology

Discover artifacts from the Neolithic era to the Roman period, including the iconic “Goddess on the Throne.”

Ethnography

Learn about the life and traditions of Albanians in Kosovo during the Ottoman period through detailed exhibits.

Natural Science

Explore Kosovo’s natural history, including geological and biological specimens from across the region.
